Breadown on SCD (Sickle Cell Disease)

Red Blood Cell

These are specialized cells in your blood that carry oxygen throughout your body. Normally, they are round and flexible, allowing them to move easily through small blood vessels.

Sickle Cell Disease

In SCD, a genetic mutation alters the hemoglobin protein inside red blood cells. Hemoglobin is responsible for carrying oxygen. The mutation causes the red blood cells to become sickle-shaped (like a crescent moon) instead of round.

Problems Caused by Sickle Cells

  • Sickle-shaped cells are rigid and sticky.
